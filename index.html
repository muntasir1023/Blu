Electrolytes are essential minerals that carry an electric charge. They are vital for your body's most basic functions, from maintaining hydration to supporting nerve and muscle performance.

Sodium is the primary electrolyte for maintaining fluid balance. It regulates blood pressure and is crucial for proper nerve and muscle function, ensuring your system remains primed for action.

Essential for cellular function, Potassium supports a steady heart rhythm and ensures efficient nerve signaling. It works in tandem with sodium to power the cellular "engines" of your body.

Magnesium is involved in over 300 biochemical reactions. It’s key for energy production, protein synthesis, and muscle relaxation, helping you recover faster and perform longer.

Beyond bone health, Calcium is vital for blood clotting, heart rhythm, and muscle contraction. It ensures the mechanical systems of your body operate with fluid efficiency.

Chloride works with other electrolytes to maintain the right balance of fluids inside and outside of cells. It’s also a key component of digestive juices and helps maintain healthy blood pressure levels.
